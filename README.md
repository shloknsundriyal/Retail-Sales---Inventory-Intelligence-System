# Retail Sales & Inventory Intelligence System

## Overview

The Retail Sales & Inventory Intelligence System is an end-to-end Business Intelligence project developed to help retail organizations transform transactional data into actionable business insights.

The solution integrates Excel, SQL, and Power BI to analyze sales performance, inventory health, customer purchasing behavior, operational efficiency, and staff productivity across multiple stores and product categories.

The project delivers a centralized analytics framework that enables business stakeholders to monitor key performance indicators, identify operational bottlenecks, optimize inventory allocation, and support data-driven decision-making.

---

## Business Problem

Retail businesses generate large volumes of transactional data from customers, products, inventory, stores, and staff operations. However, without a structured analytics system, it becomes difficult to:

- Monitor sales performance across locations
- Identify high-performing products and brands
- Detect inventory shortages before stock-outs occur
- Track shipment delays and fulfillment efficiency
- Understand customer purchasing behavior
- Evaluate staff productivity
- Support strategic business decisions with data

This project addresses these challenges by creating a scalable Retail Intelligence Dashboard capable of delivering real-time business insights.

## Dataset Architecture

The project utilizes a relational retail dataset consisting of 9 interconnected tables.

### Sales Domain
- Orders
- Order Items
- Customers
- Stores
- Staffs

### Production Domain
- Products
- Stocks
- Brands
- Categories

The dataset contains over 5,000+ retail transactions representing sales, inventory, customer, and operational activities.

## Business Insights Generated

### Revenue Performance

- Revenue was concentrated among a limited number of product categories and brands.
- Top-performing brands significantly outperformed competitors.
- Revenue trends highlighted opportunities for category-level growth optimization.

### Customer Analytics

- Customer revenue contribution was unevenly distributed across the customer base.
- Repeat purchase behavior presented opportunities for retention-focused initiatives.
- Discount dependency impacted overall revenue realization.

### Inventory Management

- Multiple products were identified as low-stock risks.
- Inventory allocation inefficiencies were observed across stores.
- Inventory turnover metrics indicated opportunities for stock optimization.

### Operations

- Shipment delays were concentrated in specific locations.
- Fulfillment bottlenecks were identified through operational analysis.

### Staff Performance

- Significant variation existed in staff sales productivity.
- Top performers generated a disproportionately large share of revenue.

## Strategic Recommendations

### Customer Retention
Develop targeted loyalty programs and personalized engagement strategies to improve repeat purchase rates and customer lifetime value.

### Inventory Optimization
Implement demand-driven inventory allocation and automated stock alerts to minimize stock-out risks.

### Operational Efficiency
Improve fulfillment workflows and logistics coordination in locations experiencing high shipment delays.

### Pricing & Discounts
Optimize discounting strategies to reduce revenue leakage while maintaining sales volume.

### Staff Development
Introduce performance benchmarking and training initiatives to improve overall sales productivity.
